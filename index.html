Christopher Marlowe

The Rebel Playwright

1564 – 1593

Early Life

  • Born in Canterbury, England, 1564 — same year as Shakespeare
  • Father was a shoemaker — the family had little money
  • Won a scholarship to King's School, then Cambridge University
  • Almost didn't get his master's degree — people thought he was a Catholic spy
  • The Queen's council stepped in: Marlowe had done "good service" for the country

Most historians believe he was working as a government spy.

The Spy Who Wrote Plays

  • Between 1587 and 1593, Marlowe changed English theater forever
  • Before Marlowe: plays used clunky rhymes or plain prose
  • Marlowe introduced blank verse — unrhymed poetry with a steady beat
  • His characters weren't saints or symbols — they were ambitious, dangerous, and real
  • In just six years, he invented the kinds of plays that Shakespeare would later make famous

His Greatest Plays

  • Tamburlaine the Great — a conqueror who stops at nothing; first full-length blank verse play
  • Doctor Faustus — a man sells his soul to the devil for knowledge and power
  • The Jew of Malta — a dark comedy about religious hypocrisy
  • Edward II — a king destroyed by his own bad choices; a political tragedy

Each play pushed the boundaries of what theater could do.

The Poet

  • Hero and Leander — a funny, romantic love story poem that doesn't take itself too seriously
  • The Passionate Shepherd to His Love — one of the most famous poems of the entire era
  • The shepherd promises his lover a perfect life of flowers and beauty
  • Other poets wrote responses — Ralegh's "Nymph's Reply" says: "Sounds nice, but it won't last"
  • His translation of Ovid's poems was so bold the church ordered it burned

A Dangerous Reputation

  • Marlowe was as bold in life as the characters he created
  • A man named Richard Baines wrote down a list of shocking things Marlowe supposedly said:
    • That Jesus wasn't real
    • That religion was just a way to control people
  • If taken to court, these charges could have meant execution
  • He was arrested multiple times but always avoided serious punishment
  • Probably because powerful people were protecting him

A Mysterious Death

  • May 30, 1593 — Marlowe was just 29 years old
  • Spent the day at a boarding house in Deptford with three men tied to the spy world
  • Official story: an argument over the dinner bill
  • Ingram Frizer stabbed Marlowe above the right eye, killing him
  • Frizer claimed self-defense and was pardoned within weeks
  • Many believe the murder was connected to his spying activities

We may never know the full truth.

His Legacy

Marlowe died young, but his impact was enormous.

Shakespeare's early plays borrowed heavily from Marlowe's style and rhythm.

Without Marlowe, the golden age of English theater
might never have happened at all.
