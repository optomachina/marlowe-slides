Christopher Marlowe

The Rebel Playwright

1564 – 1593

Early Life

  • Born in Canterbury, England, 1564 — same year as Shakespeare
  • Father was a shoemaker — the family had little money
  • Won a scholarship to King's School, then Cambridge
  • Almost didn't get his degree — people thought he was a Catholic spy
  • The Queen's council stepped in: Marlowe had done "good service"

Most historians believe he was working as a government spy.

Canterbury Cathedral

The Spy Who Wrote Plays

  • Between 1587 and 1593, Marlowe changed English theater forever
  • Before Marlowe: plays used clunky rhymes or plain prose
  • Marlowe introduced blank verse — unrhymed poetry with a steady beat
  • His characters were ambitious, dangerous, and real
  • In just six years, he invented the plays Shakespeare later made famous

The Rose Theatre excavation

His Greatest Plays

  • Tamburlaine the Great — a conqueror who stops at nothing
  • Doctor Faustus — a man sells his soul to the devil
  • The Jew of Malta — a dark comedy about religious hypocrisy
  • Edward II — a king destroyed by his own bad choices

Each play pushed the boundaries of what theater could do.

Doctor Faustus (1631)

The Poet

  • Hero and Leander — a funny, romantic love story poem
  • The Passionate Shepherd — one of the most famous poems of the era
  • The shepherd promises his lover a perfect life of flowers
  • Ralegh's "Nymph's Reply": "Sounds nice, but it won't last"
  • His Ovid translation was so bold the church ordered it burned

Ovid — Marlowe translated his poems

A Dangerous Reputation

  • Marlowe was as bold in life as the characters he created
  • Richard Baines wrote a list of shocking things Marlowe supposedly said:
    • That Jesus wasn't real
    • That religion was just a way to control people
  • These charges could have meant execution
  • He was arrested multiple times but always avoided punishment
  • Probably because powerful people were protecting him

Elizabeth I — whose council protected Marlowe

A Mysterious Death

  • May 30, 1593 — Marlowe was just 29 years old
  • Spent the day at a boarding house in Deptford with three men tied to the spy world
  • Official story: an argument over the dinner bill
  • Ingram Frizer stabbed Marlowe above the right eye
  • Frizer claimed self-defense and was pardoned within weeks
  • Many believe the murder was tied to his spying activities

We may never know the full truth.

Marlowe memorial, Deptford

His Legacy

Marlowe died young, but his impact was enormous.

Shakespeare's early plays borrowed heavily from Marlowe's style and rhythm.

Without Marlowe, the golden age of English theater
might never have happened at all.
